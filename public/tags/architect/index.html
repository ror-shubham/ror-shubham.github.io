Architect

Sales Intelligence

6sense acquired a company named Slintel. Slintel and legacy 6sense Sales Intelligence were merged to form new Sales Intelligence app I was part of 3 member architect team responsible for taking technology decisions which included creating boilerplate to connect with our backend, switch to typescript from javascript, using craco framework, porting reusable front components from legacy app. The ideation started with the problem statement of merging the two apps. We started thinking of just adding one app’s Frontend as an iframe in the other app, but instead we ended up with a total UI overhaul, complete new design language and major changes in architecture. This was a big project which took 5 months and 15 engineers and a lot of late nights. The app was a huge success bringing in 20 million+ revenue

Sales Dashboards

OrangeSomething shut down its operations soon after Covid lockdowns. My journey with 6sense started soon thereafter. My first project was to create a new app named Sales Dashboards from Scratch. I led the frontend development of this app. I was sole Frontend developer responsible for creating the Frontend architecture, setting up boilerplate code, setting up the CI/CD pipeline, and creating the initial set of reusable components. Two other developers joined later to help with the development. The architecture we chose was Javascript + React + GraphQL + Apollo. The backend was built with Django Developing this app was a great experience where we spent 3 months in hackathon mode and were pushing patches till the last minute. The app went live in November 2020, and I was responsible for the maintaining and supporting the Frontend for the next 4.5 years till it was shut down in March 2025. RIP 🪦 The app was a starting point for the sales team to get insights and recommendations for their accounts. It was a single page app with multiple tabs.
